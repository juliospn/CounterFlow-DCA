What is DCA?

Dollar Cost Averaging (DCA) is an investment strategy that involves the regular purchase of assets at regular intervals. This helps to reduce the impact of market fluctuations over the long term.

DCA with Market Sentiment Focus

The DCA strategy with a focus on market sentiment is based on data that shows the opinion and trends of the market. The idea is to buy more during periods of upward trend in the market and less during periods of downward trend.

Benefits of the DCA Strategy with a Focus on Market Sentiment

  • Reduces the impact of market fluctuations over the long term
  • Capitalizes on market opportunities by buying more during upward trends and less during downward trends
  • Helps to maximize your returns over the long term

Comparison with "Buy the Dip" strategy

The "Buy the Dip" strategy involves buying assets during market downturns, with the expectation that the price will recover. While this may work in some cases, the DCA strategy with a focus on market sentiment offers a more balanced and consistent approach.

By using the DCA strategy with a focus on market sentiment, you take advantage of market opportunities regardless of the market direction. You don't have to wait for a market dip to start investing. This means you can start investing now and reap the benefits over time.

Reconciling with the MVRV Indicator

The MRVR (Market Value to Realized Value) indicator is an analytical tool that has been used to study the Bitcoin cycle. This indicator was proposed by Murad Mahmudov and David Puell in 2018 and is based on the relationship between the current market value of Bitcoin and the realized market value (or "realized") of Bitcoin.

The MRVR is used to analyze the overvaluation or undervaluation of Bitcoin relative to its historical value, which is represented by the realized market value. When the MRVR is greater than 3.5, this may indicate a possible overvaluation of Bitcoin and a possible price peak on the horizon. On the other hand, when the MRVR is less than 1.0, it may indicate a possible undervaluation of Bitcoin and a buying opportunity.

What is the Funding Rate?

The Bitcoin funding rate is a financing rate that traders pay or receive when trading Bitcoin futures contracts. If the funding rate is positive, traders who are buying the futures contract pay traders who are selling. If the funding rate is negative, traders who are selling pay those who are buying.

In Bitcoin price rallies, the funding rate tends to be positive, as there are more traders buying than selling futures contracts. This can indicate that there is more optimism in the market about the future price of Bitcoin. In price declines, the funding rate tends to be negative, indicating that there are more traders selling than buying, which may signal a more pessimistic sentiment about the future price of Bitcoin. However, the funding rate can vary depending on the timing and behavior of the market, so it's important to analyze it alongside other indicators to make informed trading decisions.

How to Implement the Strategy

The funding rate strategy with DCA and the MRVR indicator involves first using the MRVR indicator to understand which phase of the Bitcoin cycle you are in. If the MRVR is in the region of 1, it is ideal to make small and constant investments whenever the triggers of very high funding rates (at negative levels) appear. These triggers signal a phase of oversold cycle in the spot market, which is a great long-term buying opportunity.

In addition, the same strategy can be used when the Bitcoin price enters the overbought part of the cycle. In this case, it is necessary to analyze the funding rate together with MRVR and make small sales to generate cash when the oversold period comes again.
