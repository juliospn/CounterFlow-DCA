DCA with Market Sentiment Focus

The strategy of regular purchases at Bitcoin's oversold points involves making small and consistent investments, going against the herd at the moment of exhaustion of the downward trend.

What is DCA?

Dollar Cost Averaging (DCA) is an investment strategy that involves regularly buying assets at fixed intervals. This helps reduce the impact of market fluctuations in the long run, especially when dealing with a volatile asset like Bitcoin. DCA reduces emotional swings and requires no market or price monitoring.

It is known to be the safest and most profitable strategy in the long term.

DCA with Market Sentiment Focus

The DCA (Dollar Cost Averaging) strategy with a focus on market sentiment is about combining the advantages of regular buying with data that shows the sentiment and trends of the market.

The idea is to buy more during periods of exhaustion of the downward trend and take profits in regions of exhaustion of the upward trend.

Benefits of the DCA Strategy with a Focus on Market Sentiment

  • ⚬ Reduces mood swings in the face of price volatility.
  • ⚬ Can take advantage of market opportunities as they arise.
  • ⚬ Helps maximize long-term returns.
  • ⚬ By buying Bitcoin and withdrawing it from exchanges, investors will be contributing to the health of the market.
  • ⚬ A well-founded and antifragile strategy, with practice and results, the investor feels increasingly comfortable and accustomed.

Comparison with "Buy the Dip" strategy

In the traditional market, especially in the American stock market, which is the most liquid and valuable in the world, since the breaking of the gold standard in 1971, the stock market has been highly correlated to financial stimulus. There are corrections and drops, but the S&P 500 index is always rising, even after deep crises such as those of 2008 and 2000. This has allowed investors to abuse "reckless" strategies that make sense in this majority bullish scenario.

One of these "reckless" strategies that work well only in bullish markets is "Buy the Dip" or even the famous "Buy and Hold". However, in the Bitcoin market, which is highly volatile and cyclical, these strategies do not work well. The image above shows a study that compared the "Buy the Dip" strategy with the DCA strategy and showed that, even miraculously knowing the "bottom" of market drops in the traditional market, the DCA strategy still outperformed the "Buy the Dip" strategy.

In other words, in most situations, the concern and stress of trying to time the bottom is not worth it. For the emotional well-being of the investor and even in terms of performance, the DCA strategy ends up being much better, as it is not necessary to worry about market prices and news, only small and constant investments.

It is from this analysis that a mixed strategy emerges, which takes advantage of and combines the benefits of each strategy, especially for the Bitcoin market, which does not only experience growth but also cycles.

Combining with the NUPL Indicator

The key principle of this tool lies in the relationship between Bitcoin market capitalization and investors taking profits.

Market value increases much faster than profit realization, indicating that the market is overheating, possibly due to investor greed (red zone). For the strategic investor, these times have historically been favorable for profit-taking.

We can divide different percentages of Bitcoin Unrealized Profit/Loss Relative to determine which stage of the market we are in. This can be advantageous for the long-term strategic investor.

The NUPL tool assists the investor in identifying which stage of the Bitcoin cycle they are in, which can be combined with the DCA strategy to take advantage of opportunities during downtrends.

What is the Funding Rate?

To understand the Funding Rate, it is important to know that a huge percentage of Bitcoin trades are made on the Futures Market. Therefore, the sentiment of Futures Market investors ends up being very relevant and impactful on the final price behavior of Bitcoin.

The Bitcoin funding rate is a financing rate that traders pay or receive when trading Bitcoin futures contracts. If the funding rate is positive, traders who are buying pay this funding rate to traders who are selling. If the funding rate is negative, traders who are selling pay to those who are buying.

During Bitcoin price rallies, the funding rate tends to be positive as there are more traders buying than selling futures contracts. This can indicate more optimism in the market regarding the future price of Bitcoin. In price declines, the funding rate tends to be negative, indicating that there are more traders selling than buying, which can signal a more pessimistic sentiment regarding the future price of Bitcoin.

How to Implement the Strategy

Less is more. "Every four years" a cycle is initiated due to the halving, which causes price highs and then corrections. The DCA strategy with Funding Rate and NUPL involves first using the NUPL indicator to understand which stage of the Bitcoin cycle we are in.

If NUPL is below 20%, the ideal is to make small and constant investments whenever funding rate triggers appear. These triggers indicate a low cycle phase in the oversold region, which can be a great buying opportunity in the medium and long term.

In addition, it is possible to use the same strategy when the price of Bitcoin enters the overbought part of the cycle. In this case, NUPL and Funding Rate are also used to make small sales with the intention of accumulating cash for when the oversold period returns.
